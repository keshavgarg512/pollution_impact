What You Can Do to Help Pollinators

There are many ways you can contribute to reducing pollution and protecting pollinators. Here are some impactful steps you can take to make a difference.

Plant a Pollinator-Friendly Garden

Grow native plants and flowers that attract pollinators. These provide essential food and shelter for bees, butterflies, and other pollinators.

Reduce Pesticide Use

Avoid using harmful pesticides and opt for organic gardening methods. Pesticides can harm pollinators and damage their habitats.

Support Local, Organic Farms

Buying from organic farms reduces demand for pesticide-heavy farming. Local farms also often adopt pollinator-friendly practices.

Educate Your Community

Spread awareness about the importance of pollinators and the dangers of pollution. Organize community events to make a collective impact.

Create Bee Habitats

Build or install bee hotels to provide shelter for native bee species. This helps increase bee populations and support local ecosystems.

Reduce Light Pollution

Use outdoor lights minimally or with timers. Reducing light pollution protects nocturnal pollinators like moths from habitat disruption.
