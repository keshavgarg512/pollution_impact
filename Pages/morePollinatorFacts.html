More Pollinator Facts

Pollinators are Critical for Food Production

Pollinators are responsible for pollinating over 75% of flowering plants, including 35% of the food we eat. Without them, many of the fruits, vegetables, and nuts we depend on would cease to exist.

Bees are the Most Efficient Pollinators

Bees, particularly honeybees, are some of the most efficient pollinators. They play a crucial role in food production and are essential for the reproduction of many plants.

Pollinators Help Maintain Biodiversity

By pollinating plants, pollinators ensure the survival of plant species that support various other forms of wildlife. This biodiversity is vital for ecosystem stability.

Threats to Pollinators are Growing

Pollinators face increasing threats from habitat loss, pesticide use, climate change, and air and water pollution, all of which harm their populations and disrupt their natural behavior.

Humans Depend on Pollinators More Than We Realize

Many of our favorite foods, like apples, almonds, and blueberries, rely on pollinators to produce the crops. The loss of pollinators could lead to a major reduction in food availability and variety.
