Global and Indian Schemes for Pollinator Protection

Various international and national programs have been initiated to protect pollinators and support biodiversity. These programs focus on reducing pollution, conserving habitats, and promoting sustainable practices. Here are some significant initiatives:

Global Schemes

  • International Pollinator Initiative (IPI): Led by the UN, the IPI promotes global collaboration to protect pollinator health through policy-making and research.
  • The Global Pollination Project: This project supports the conservation of pollinators in diverse regions, aiming to increase pollinator populations.
  • UN Environment Programme's Pollinator Protection Plan: Focuses on reducing pesticide use and protecting pollinator habitats.

Indian Schemes

  • National Mission for Sustainable Agriculture (NMSA): This scheme promotes organic farming practices to protect pollinators from harmful chemicals.
  • Pollinator Habitat Enhancement in Agriculture: Encourages habitat conservation in agricultural regions to support local pollinator populations.
  • Indian Pollinator Conservation Project: A collaborative project focused on monitoring and conserving native pollinators across different Indian states.
