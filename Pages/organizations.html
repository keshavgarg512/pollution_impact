Introduction

Pollinators like bees, butterflies, birds, and bats play a vital role in maintaining biodiversity and food security by pollinating plants and crops. Unfortunately, these essential creatures are facing numerous threats, including habitat loss, pesticide exposure, and climate change. Many organizations are working tirelessly to protect and restore pollinator populations. Below are some of the key organizations dedicated to saving pollinators.

Pollinator Partnership

The Pollinator Partnership is a nonprofit organization focused on protecting and promoting the health of pollinators and their ecosystems. They work on various initiatives, such as creating pollinator-friendly habitats, educating the public, and advocating for policies that support pollinator health.

Visit Website

The Xerces Society

The Xerces Society is dedicated to the conservation of invertebrates, including pollinators like bees, butterflies, and moths. They provide science-based resources, run campaigns to protect pollinators, and advocate for policies that improve pollinator habitats.

Visit Website

Bee City USA

Bee City USA is a program that encourages communities to create pollinator-friendly spaces. They focus on increasing awareness, creating habitats, and engaging local governments and citizens in the conservation of pollinators.

Visit Website

Friends of the Earth

Friends of the Earth is an international network of environmental organizations advocating for sustainable policies, ecosystems, and pollinators. They work to promote the protection of the environment and support initiatives to save pollinators globally.
